Welcome

While I was born in Calgary, Alberta and have spent most of my life in Canada, sometimes I like to pretend I’m from California. My family and I moved down to the Bay Area for a total of four years when I was in elementary school before returning back to my hometown of Cochrane—a small town between Calgary and the mountains.

Having spent time on the West Coast at such a pivotal time in my life, I set out to attend the Sauder School of Business at UBC in Vancouver immediately after finishing high school. While the ocean, mountains, and West Coast lifestyle were the driving factors in my move to this city, I still took full advantage of my time at UBC.

Over the course of my four-year Bachelor of Commerce degree, I learned how to work with and lead a team, underwrite an office/retail/residential building for investment, use Python to determine whether companies with attractive CEOs outperform companies with less attractive CEOs, and learned that I have a much bigger interest in animal welfare than I ever realized.

Immediately after finishing my education at UBC, I cycled from the house I lived at throughout university to the border of Mexico near San Diego. Shortly after, I traded my bike for swim shorts and left to travel Maui for two weeks with my girlfriend and her family. About a week after returning from Maui, my girlfriend and I then packed our bags and were off to backpack through Europe for the next 7 weeks. Needless to say, I had a pretty fantastic summer.

Currently, I am doing contract work remotely for a small technology company based out of Calgary called SchoolSoft Technologies. As this contract winds down I will be actively looking for opportunities to get involved in the real estate, consulting, or technology industries in Vancouver. If you’d like to reach out, or simply have some suggestions for how I can improve my website, contact me at logan@loganparker.ca.

After cycling for 23 days and 2,026km, I finally made it from Vancouver to San Francisco. Only 12 days and 1,090km to go until the border of Mexico!

Interests

Cycling

From May to mid-June of 2016, I cycled from Vancouver, British Columbia down to the border of Mexico near San Diego. This journey totalled 3,100km and took 33 days of cycling. I biked from Vancouver to the border of California and Oregon with my friend Tanner, and cycled the remaining distance alone. I camped along the way, carrying everything I needed for the 5 week venture on my bicycle.

Climbing

From about mid March to late September--and on the occassional nice day during the winter--the North Shore mountains all the way to Whistler and beyond present incredible terrain for climbing. Whenever possible, I load up a car with friends and gear and drive north to the mountains. Currently I enjoy bouldering, top roping, and sport climbing. By the spring of 2018 I am hoping to get into trad climbing!

Skiing

Having grown up only 45 minutes from the Rocky Mountains, I have skied nearly my entire life. I love getting out into the mountains and taking a break from the city. Currently, you can find me up at Whistler Blackcomb skiing in the park, or out on the mountain with my fat skis!

Surfing

In September of 1999, my family and I moved from Calgary, Alberta to Saratoga, California. Living only 40 minutes from Santa Cruz, I learned to surf on weekends from the age of 5-9. Since then, I continue to surf on Vancouver Island at Tofino and Sombrio.
